Having a professional online presence is no longer optional for businesses in Pakistan. Whether you run a consulting firm in Karachi, a medical clinic in Islamabad, or a retail brand in Lahore, a website is your digital storefront.

But building a website can feel overwhelming. Many business owners end up spending too much money on features they don't need, or missing critical essentials. Here is a practical checklist of what your website actually needs.

### 1. Reliable Local Domain & Fast Hosting
- **Get a .pk or .com.pk Domain:** Using a local domain helps you build local trust and search relevance. Register it via PKNIC or accredited local resellers.
- **Fast Global CDN Hosting:** Traditional shared hosting in Pakistan can be slow. Hosting your static site on modern networks like Cloudflare or Vercel ensures your pages load in under a second on local mobile connections (Jazz/Zong).

### 2. Direct WhatsApp Communication Button
Most consumers in Pakistan prefer communicating via WhatsApp rather than sending emails or filling out long forms.
- A floating WhatsApp chat badge should be visible on every page.
- Pre-fill the WhatsApp link with a custom message so you know exactly which page the customer came from.

### 3. Clear Mobile-Responsive Design
Over 80% of your web traffic in Pakistan will come from mobile phones.
- Ensure your menu works smoothly on small screens.
- Avoid large images that eat up mobile data limits.
- Place phone numbers and physical address links clearly on the header and footer.

### 4. Direct Landing Page Action
Every page on your website should have a singular, clear action you want the customer to take. Whether it is "Call Now," "Book an Appointment," or "View Catalog," make sure it is prominently displayed in a contrasting color.
